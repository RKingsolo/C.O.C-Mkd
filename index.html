About Our Church

Our History

Established on the 17th Day of May 1978 by Late. Bro. Paul O. Oputa (Evangelist), the Church of Christ at No.5 Owerri Street, High Level, Makurdi, Benue State, Nigeria; has been a beacon of hope and faith in our community for over four decades. We started as a small gathering of believers meeting in a rented space through outreach Evangelism programs and have grown by God's grace to become one of the most vibrant congregations of the Lord in the region.

Our journey has been marked by faithful service, biblical teaching, and a commitment to spreading the Gospel in its purest form, which has brought about the establishment of more than one hundred and seventy five (175) congregations across Benue State, Nigeria.

Our Affirmations

Our Core Beliefs

The Bible

We believe the Bible is the inspired and authoritative Word of God, the only infallible and all-sufficient rule of faith and practice for Christians in the 21st century and beyond. It is complete and sufficient for teaching, reproof, correction, and training in righteousness (2 Timothy 3:16-17).

Jesus Christ

We believe in Jesus Christ as the Son of God, our only Savior, Redeemer, Messiah and Lord. Through His death, burial and resurrection, we have salvation and the promise of eternal life, if we remain faithful till the end. He is the Head of the church and our mediator with God (1 Timothy 2:5; Colossians 1:13-18).

Salvation & Baptism

We believe salvation comes by grace through faith In Christ Jesus (Ephesians 2:8-9), expressed through Godly sorrow that leads to repentance and baptism by immersion for the forgiveness of sins (Acts 2:38). Baptism is our response to God's grace, not a work of merit but an act of faith in Christ Jesus Our Lord.

The Church

We believe the church is the body of Christ on earth, purchased by Christ Jesus and built upon the confession that He is the Christ, the Son of the Living God (Matthew 16:18). The church is autonomous in her governance, following the New Testament pattern for administration, organization and worship.

Communion

We observe the Lord's Supper upon every first day of the week as a memorial of Christ's sacrifice on the cross (Acts 20:7). The bread represents His body, and the cup represents His blood shed for our redemption, sanctification and salvation.

Christian Living

We believe Christians are called to live holy lives, distinct from the world (Romans 12:1-2). This includes loving God with all our heart, soul, mind, and strength, and loving our neighbors as ourselves and not hearers alone but doers of the Word of God (Mark 12:30-31).

Christ's Return

We believe in the personal and imminent return of Christ Jesus, who will judge the living and the dead (2 Timothy 4:1). He will receive believers unto Himself and establish His eternal kingdom: The prepared place for the prepared people of God (1 Thessalonians 4:16-17; Colossians 3:1-4; Revelation 21:1-4).
